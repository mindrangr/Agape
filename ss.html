Recruiting: Meet the Miami Hurricanes early enrollees

The Hurricanes won’t play a football game again until September. Spring practice is still a couple of months away. And though it looms ever closer, there are still a few weeks until National Signing Day.

But in a way, a new season began Monday for the Hurricanes as they returned to campus for a new semester, began classes and welcomed five early enrollees – players they hope will be able to make a difference for them sooner rather than later.

Among the early enrollees? A quarterback, a legacy and three linebackers – a position where Miami needed some depth this year after injuries to Raphael Kirby and Darrion Owens.

Here’s a look at the new Hurricanes that have made their way onto Miami’s roster and will be getting their first chance to put on orange and green when Miami opens spring practice in March.

Name: Jack Allison

Position: Quarterback

Height/weight: 6-5, 200

High school: Palmetto High (Palmetto)

247Sports.com composite star rating: Four

The Buzz: An Under Armour All-American, Allison is one of the top pro-style quarterback prospects in the nation. He completed 125 of 201 passes for 1,450 yards with 11 touchdowns against just five interceptions as a senior and passed for another 1,600 yards his junior year. He’s been one of the longest committed prospects in Miami’s 2016 class and has the size and touch college coaches covet in prospects. After signing a grant-in-aid agreement with Miami, he earned a lot of praise from former Hurricanes offensive coordinator James Coley, who spoke highly of Allison’s tenacity and toughness playing behind a relatively young, inexperienced line. With Miami returning quarterbacks Brad Kaaya, Malik Rosier, Vincent Testaverde and Evan Shirreffs next season, Allison will likely redshirt – something he told the Tampa Bay Times he expects. “I’m just going to get there and develop and become a better quarterback.”

(Matt Murschel)

Name: Patrick Bethel

Position: Defensive end

Height/weight: 6-3, 249

High school: Vero Beach High (Vero Beach)

247Sports.com composite star rating: Four

247Sports.com national composite ranking: 225

247Sports.com position composite ranking: 11

247Sports.com state composite ranking: 34

The Buzz: Another Under Armour All-American, Bethel is the son of former Hurricanes tight end Randy Bethel. But the younger Bethel has made it clear he intends to forge his own path at Miami, writing when he verbally committed that, “I chose Miami to create my own destiny, to provide the best for myself, and most importantly, to bring back that lost Swagger.” Known as a solid pass rusher with the speed to get in the backfield quickly, Bethel had nine sacks as a senior and was rated the top prospect on the Treasure Coast in the TCPalm Super 11 rankings.

Name: Zach McCloud

Position: Linebacker

Height/weight: 6-2, 218

High school: Santaluces High (Lake Worth)

247Sports.com composite star rating: Four

247Sports.com national composite ranking: 255

247Sports.com position composite ranking: 19

247Sports.com state composite ranking: 37

The Buzz: A first-team Sun Sentinel All-County selection this past season, McCloud had pledged to the Hurricanes, but backed off his verbal commitment after the firing of former coach Al Golden. Still, he never ruled Miami out and after a December visit that included meeting with new coach Mark Richt, the linebacker – with plenty of size, speed, and power – rejoined the Hurricanes recruiting class. He held offers from some of the biggest programs in college football including Alabama, Auburn, Florida, Florida State and Richt’s former program – Georgia – as well as several others.

Name: Michael Pinckney

Position: Linebacker

Height/weight: 6-1, 215

High school: Raines (Jacksonville)

247Sports.com composite star rating: Three

247Sports.com national composite ranking: 501

247Sports.com position composite ranking: 19

247Sports.com state composite ranking: 72

The Buzz: Another player at a position of need for the Hurricanes, Pinckney finished his senior season with 85 tackles, eight sacks and an interception. That followed a junior season in which he notched 75 tackles. A longtime Miami commit, he was named to the Jacksonville Times Union’s Super 24 team and signed a grant-in-aid agreement with the Hurricanes back in August. He committed in 2014 and helped lead Raines to the Class 4A state championship game.

Name: Shaquille Quarterman

Position: Linebacker

Height/weight: 6-0, 233

High school: Oakleaf High (Oak Park, FL)

247Sports.com composite star rating: Four

247Sports.com national composite ranking: 146

247Sports.com position composite ranking: 7

247Sports.com state composite ranking: 22

The Buzz: A U.S. Army All-American, Quarterman is physical and especially strong against the run. He was rated one of the top 20 prospects in the state by the Orlando Sentinel and is from the same high school that produced Owens, who is expected to have a strong showing next season after returning from a knee injury. Quarterman has been a longtime Miami commit and is known for his hard hits and he’s only improved since his freshman season in which he registered 80 tackles.
